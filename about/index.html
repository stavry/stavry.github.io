Hello and welcome to stavry!

stavry is short for Srivastava + Gregory, named after the creators of this site: Aditya Srivastava and Rachel Gregory.

We’re partners who realized in December 2021 that we wanted a new project to work on. 2021 was a year full of voracious reading for both of us, which eventually made us want to write. We started thinking about how we would want to explore this new interest– technical blogs? novels? short stories? non fiction? – and settled on why not all of the above? A joint website where we could write about the things we do, create, and consume seemed to be the perfect project.

Around the same time, Aditya started eagerly perusing Berkeley (our alma mater)’s course catalog to look for subjects he wanted to explore while in school, but didn’t have the bandwidth for while completing a double major in Computer Sciece and Molecular & Cellular Biology. The list started getting long because there’s so much awesome stuff to learn out there! So, we decided to add a page for learning so that we can keep track of learning resources for each subject we want to study.

rachel: github | instagram

Hi! I’m a software engineer who works on core infrastructure at DoorDash. I’m originally from Nashville, but have lived in the Bay Area since 2017.

I’ve always had a lot of different things I was interested in now that I think about it. As a kid I sang, read, wrote, danced, and played basketball. In middle school, I dropped dance to try basically every sport I could: soccer, cheerleading, basketball, softball, cross country, track. Then in high school, I dropped all those stressful team sports and narrowed it down to cross country and track. But all throughout, singing and writing never left.

In college, I struggled a lot with having enough time to do all the things I wanted to, because most of my time was spent on Computer Science, Linguistics, Chinese, and working a part-time job. I sang for one year with an a capella group on campus, but eventually had to leave that because of the time commitment.

So after I graduated in December 2020, I knew I needed to reconnect with those parts of me that I had neglected for a few years. I slowly started exercising again, reading again, exploring new music, and always looked to learn something new without any performance pressure. It was beautiful and healing and I’m thankful that I’ve been able to carry that energy with me as I started work this past August.

Currently I’m focusing on…
art: crocheting, writing
learning: reading, Hindi
athletics: rock climbing, running

In the next year or so, I hope to make headway on my novel, crochet a sweater, read a lot of awesome books, gain elementary proficiency in Hindi, run a 5k in less than 25 minutes, and be able to send most V5s I encounter.

But I also really like…
art: singing, piano, drawing, watercoloring

athletics: hiking, badmintion, frisbee, cycling
